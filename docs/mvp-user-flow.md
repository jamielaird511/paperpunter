# PaperPunter MVP User Flow

## Purpose

PaperPunter exists to make running tipping competitions effortless.

The product should feel like:

> “I set this up in 2 minutes and now everyone thinks I’m organised.”

The owner gets the office kudos.
PaperPunter does the admin.

---

# Core Product Principle

Every decision should support:

- low friction
- fast setup
- ongoing engagement
- minimal admin
- mobile-first usability
- social banter

The MVP should feel opinionated, simple, and alive.

Not corporate.
Not over-engineered.
Not spreadsheet-like.

---

# Primary User Types

## Competition Owner

The person running the competition.

Usually:
- office organiser
- tradie
- sales manager
- club organiser
- sports fan in a group chat

What they want:
- easy setup
- no manual scoring
- no chasing picks
- weekly engagement
- social credit

What they do NOT want:
- complex settings
- spreadsheets
- admin work
- technical setup

---

## Participant

The person entering the competition.

What they want:
- fast signup
- quick picks
- leaderboard updates
- banter
- mobile-friendly experience

The participant experience should feel:
- casual
- fast
- addictive
- socially engaging

---

# MVP User Journey

## 1. Landing Page

User arrives on PaperPunter homepage.

Primary CTA:
- Create Competition

Secondary CTA:
- Join Competition

The landing page should communicate:
- easy setup
- zero admin
- social engagement
- office/mates atmosphere

---

## 2. Account Creation

User signs up with:
- email
- password
- display name

No complicated onboarding.

After signup:
→ redirect directly into competition creation.

---

## 3. Create Competition

Owner selects:
- sport/template
- competition name
- privacy type
- scoring defaults

PaperPunter automatically handles:
- fixtures
- rounds
- scoring
- leaderboard
- reminders

The setup flow should take:
< 2 minutes.

---

# Initial Competition Templates

MVP templates may include:
- AFL tipping
- NRL tipping
- Super Rugby tipping
- FIFA World Cup
- Generic knockout tournament

Templates should be:
- opinionated
- standardized
- low-customization initially

---

## 4. Invite Participants

Owner can:
- copy invite link
- share invite code
- email invite link

The owner should never need to:
- manually add users
- manage spreadsheets
- manually score results

---

## 5. Join Competition Flow

Participant:
- opens invite link
- signs up or logs in
- automatically joins competition

Then:
→ lands directly on picks page.

The process should feel instant.

---

## 6. Picks Experience

User sees:
- upcoming fixtures
- picks
- deadlines
- leaderboard
- recent movement

Mobile-first is mandatory.

Users should be able to:
- submit picks in under 30 seconds

---

## 7. Competition Feel

The competition should feel alive.

Examples:
- leaderboard movement
- weekly reminders
- “18 of 24 tipped”
- recent results
- light social energy

Eventually:
- Gazza emails
- AI-generated banter
- weekly wrap-ups
- upset predictions

But NOT in MVP initially.

---

## 8. Weekly Automation

PaperPunter automatically:
- scores rounds
- updates ladder
- locks fixtures
- sends reminders
- tracks standings

The owner should not need to do ongoing admin.

---

# MVP Success Criteria

A competition owner can:
- create a competition quickly
- invite participants easily
- avoid spreadsheets entirely
- keep people engaged weekly

Participants:
- return weekly
- submit picks quickly
- care about leaderboard movement
- talk about the competition

---

# Explicit MVP Non-Goals

Do NOT build yet:
- payments
- betting integrations
- complex scoring builders
- advanced customization
- AI-heavy systems
- public social feeds
- marketplace/community systems
- multi-sport admin dashboards
- advanced analytics

Keep the MVP:
- focused
- opinionated
- lightweight
- easy to understand

---

# Long-Term Vision

PaperPunter becomes:
- the easiest way to run private sports competitions
- an office/social ritual product
- a low-admin engagement platform
- a weekly habit product

The product should eventually feel:
- fun
- social
- reliable
- slightly chaotic in personality
- powered by recurring banter and engagement

Gazza becomes the personality layer later.

But the utility foundation must come first.
