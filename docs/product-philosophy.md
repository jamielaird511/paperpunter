# PaperPunter V2

## Vision

PaperPunter is a low-admin sports tipping platform designed for offices, mate groups, pubs, and social communities.

Its core promise is:

> “Office kudos with zero admin.”

The organiser gets the social upside of running a great comp without the manual workload.

PaperPunter should make it incredibly easy to create and run tipping competitions that keep people connected, engaged, and talking throughout a season or tournament.

---

## Core Users

### Primary Users
- Small businesses
- Tradie offices
- Construction companies
- Car dealerships
- Sales teams
- Mate groups
- Social sports groups

### Secondary Users
- Pubs
- Community groups
- Social clubs
- Casual sports fans

These users are not looking for advanced fantasy sports features. They want something simple, social, and fun that requires minimal admin.

---

## Product Principles

### 1. Low friction beats flexibility
Easy setup and clear defaults are more important than endless customisation.

### 2. Mobile-first always
Most users will interact with PaperPunter on their phones.

### 3. The organiser is the customer
The organiser creates the competition, invites users, drives engagement, and eventually becomes the paying customer.

Every feature should reduce organiser effort while increasing organiser status.

### 4. Competitions are the centre of the system
Everything revolves around competitions.

Sports, leagues, fixtures, picks, leaderboards, and emails all exist to support competitions.

### 5. Personality matters
PaperPunter should feel alive, social, and entertaining.

It should feel more like a fun weekly ritual than a corporate tool.

### 6. Gazza is engagement, not infrastructure
Gazza enhances the experience through emails, commentary, predictions, and humour.

Gazza should never control core competition logic or platform architecture.

### 7. Opinionated over overwhelming
Avoid excessive settings and complexity.

PaperPunter should guide users toward simple, proven competition formats.

### 8. Automation is part of the product
The organiser should not feel like they are manually running a spreadsheet competition.

Scoring, reminders, leaderboards, emails, and updates should happen automatically wherever possible.

---

## What PaperPunter Is NOT

PaperPunter is not:
- A fantasy sports platform
- A gambling product
- An advanced sports analytics tool
- Enterprise competition management software
- A spreadsheet replacement tool
- A highly technical sports database

The product focus is simplicity, engagement, and low admin effort.

---

## The Magical Feeling

“PaperPunter makes it ridiculously easy to run a fun comp that keeps people connected.”

The organiser should feel like:
- they are running a great competition
- people are engaged every week
- the platform handles the hard work for them

The players should feel:
- excited to check results and leaderboards
- connected to their mates or colleagues
- entertained by weekly banter and emails

## MVP Priorities

### Core Features
- Easy competition setup
- Fixture tipping competitions
- Mobile-friendly picks
- Automated scoring
- Leaderboards
- Invite system
- Public/private/unlisted competitions
- Weekly reminder emails
- Results tracking

### Engagement Features
- Gazza weekly wrap-ups
- Funny summaries and commentary
- Weekly tipping reminders
- Simple notifications

---

## Non-MVP Features

The following features are intentionally NOT priorities for V2 MVP:
- Deep analytics
- Advanced custom scoring engines
- Public competition marketplaces
- Large-scale sports API automation
- Highly complex fantasy sports mechanics
- Extensive sport-specific logic
- Betting integrations
- Live odds integrations

---

## Long-Term Direction

PaperPunter should become the easiest and most enjoyable way to run social sports competitions online.

The long-term vision is:
- multiple sports
- reusable competition templates
- automated engagement
- strong community feeling
- low organiser workload
- highly shareable weekly experiences

The platform should feel lightweight, fun, and effortless to use.
