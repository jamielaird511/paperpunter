# Landing Page Direction

## Purpose

The PaperPunter landing page should immediately communicate:

- this is easy
- this is social
- this is fun
- this removes admin effort
- this is designed for offices, mates, and clubs

The landing page is not trying to explain every feature.

Its primary goal is:
- make organisers think “this would be perfect for our office/group”
- make users feel the platform is lightweight and enjoyable
- drive users toward creating or joining a competition

---

# Core Positioning

## Main Hook

> “Office kudos. Zero admin.”

PaperPunter helps organisers run sports tipping competitions without spreadsheets, chasing picks, updating ladders, or manually sending reminders.

The organiser gets the social credit.
PaperPunter does the hard work.

---

# Target Audience

## Primary Audience

- Small businesses
- Tradie offices
- Construction companies
- Car dealerships
- Sales teams
- Groups of mates
- Sports clubs

Typical users are:
- busy
- not highly technical
- mobile-first
- socially competitive
- looking for connection and banter

---

# Emotional Positioning

PaperPunter should feel:
- social
- casual
- entertaining
- low-pressure
- sporty
- approachable

It should NOT feel:
- corporate SaaS
- gambling-heavy
- fantasy sports nerdy
- spreadsheet-like
- overly technical

The feeling should be:
> “This looks fun and easy.”

---

# Core Messaging

## Primary Value Proposition

PaperPunter makes it ridiculously easy to run sports tipping competitions.

No spreadsheets.
No chasing picks.
No manual scoring.
No admin headaches.

---

## Secondary Messaging

- Easy setup in minutes
- Mobile-friendly picks
- Automatic leaderboards
- Weekly reminders and updates
- Built for offices, mates, and clubs
- Funny weekly banter powered by Gazza

---

# Homepage Goals

The homepage should quickly answer:

1. What is this?
2. Why should I care?
3. How does it help me?
4. How do I get started?

The user should understand the product within a few seconds.

---

# Homepage Structure

## 1. Navigation

Simple top navigation:
- Logo
- Log In
- Create Competition

Avoid clutter.

---

## 2. Hero Section

Main headline:
- clear
- bold
- easy to understand

Focus on:
- effortless competition management
- social engagement
- zero admin

Possible themes:
- Office kudos
- No spreadsheet chaos
- Run comps effortlessly
- Keep the banter alive

Primary CTA:
- Create Competition

Secondary CTA:
- Join Competition

---

## 3. Social Proof / Featured Competition

Show an example competition card.

Purpose:
- make the platform feel alive
- help users understand the concept visually
- demonstrate competitions quickly

Possible examples:
- World Cup
- Super Rugby
- Office tipping comp

---

## 4. Benefits Section

Simple feature highlights:
- Easy setup
- Automated scoring
- Mobile picks
- Weekly reminders
- Leaderboards
- Gazza emails

Focus on outcomes rather than technical features.

---

## 5. Gazza Section (later)

Gazza should eventually become a major engagement feature.

The positioning is:
- funny weekly wrap-ups
- banter
- competition personality
- social stickiness

Gazza should feel like:
> “the unhinged office comp personality”

But Gazza should not dominate the homepage initially.

The core value is still:
easy competitions with low admin.

---

# Design Direction

## Visual Style

PaperPunter should feel:
- bold
- sporty
- energetic
- clean
- mobile-first

The existing blue brand direction is strong and should continue evolving.

---

## Design Avoidances

Avoid:
- overly dark betting-site aesthetics
- excessive gradients
- fantasy sports complexity
- enterprise dashboards on the homepage
- cluttered feature walls

---

# CTA Priorities

## Primary CTA

```text
Create Competition
```

This is the core action.

The organiser is the primary customer.

---

## Secondary CTA

```text
Join Competition
```

Useful for invited users entering existing comps.

---

# Tone of Voice

PaperPunter copy should feel:
- conversational
- confident
- slightly cheeky
- simple
- human

Avoid:
- buzzwords
- corporate SaaS language
- technical jargon
- overly polished marketing fluff

The tone should feel closer to:
> “your mate explaining a great office comp”

than:
> “enterprise software marketing.”

---

# Product Identity

PaperPunter is not just a sports app.

It is:
- a social ritual platform
- an office engagement tool
- a low-admin competition system
- a weekly conversation starter

The product succeeds when:
- people check leaderboards weekly
- office banter increases
- organisers feel clever for using it
- competitions feel alive without manual effort
